A Brief History Of Cleveland

July 22, 1796: Cleveland named after Moses Cleaveland.

1836: Cleveland is incorporated as a city.

1870s:Millionaire's Row built along Euclid Avenue.

1897:Eliza Bryant founds Cleveland Home of Aged Colored People.

June 1936: The Great Lakes Exposition begins.

1940s: Cleveland numbers almost 1 million in population.

July 18 to July 23 1966: Hough Riots break out.

November 1967: Cleveland becomes first major city to elect Black Mayor (Carl Stokes).

1999: Cleveland designated as Global City

2000s: Cleveland is heralded as The Comeback City.
